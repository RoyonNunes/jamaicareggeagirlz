Who Are the Jamaica Reggae Girlz?

The Jamaica women’s national football team, nicknamed the “Reggae Girlz,” is one of the top women’s national football teams in the Caribbean region, alongside Trinidad and Tobago and Haiti. In 2008, the team was disbanded after failing to advance from the group stage of Olympic Qualifying, which featured powerhouses like the United States and Mexico. The program was revived in 2014 after a six-year hiatus, finishing second at the 2014 Women’s Caribbean Cup after a 1–0 loss to Trinidad and Tobago in the final. The team is supported by ambassador Cedella Marley, daughter of Bob Marley, who has been instrumental in raising awareness, funding, and development for the team. Jamaica made history by qualifying for the FIFA Women’s World Cup for the first time in 2019, though they were eliminated in the group stage.

Cedella Marley

Cedella Marley, daughter of reggae legend Bob Marley, is a driving force behind the success of the Jamaica Reggae Girlz. As the team's ambassador, she has:

  • Advocated for the team's reinstatement after its disbandment in 2008.
  • Raised funds for training, travel, and development programs.
  • Promoted gender equality and women's empowerment through sports.
  • Helped the team qualify for their first FIFA Women’s World Cup in 2019.
  • Continued to support the team's growth on the international stage.

Khadija "Bunny" Shaw

Khadija "Bunny" Shaw, the captain of the Jamaica Reggae Girlz, is one of the most prolific strikers in women's football. Her achievements include:

  • Top scorer for Jamaica in the 2019 FIFA Women’s World Cup qualifiers.
  • Played for top clubs like Bordeaux (France) and Manchester City (England).
  • Named CONCACAF Women’s Player of the Year in 2022.
  • Scored over 50 goals for the Jamaican national team.
  • Led the Reggae Girlz to historic World Cup appearances in 2019 and 2023.
